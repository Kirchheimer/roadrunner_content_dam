Why WCAG Compliance Matters Now
New US Regulations Are Here
Federal and state mandates now require websites to meet WCAG standards. Non-compliance can result in lawsuits, steep fines, and damage to your brand reputation. Organizations across industries—from retail to healthcare to education—must ensure their digital properties are accessible to all users.
The Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) applies to websites, and enforcement actions are increasing rapidly. Don't wait until you receive a demand letter or face legal action.
The Cost of Non-Compliance
Legal settlements for accessibility violations typically range from $5,000 to $75,000, with some cases reaching six figures. Beyond monetary penalties, organizations face reputational damage, lost customers, and reduced market reach.
WCAG Scanner helps you avoid these risks by providing continuous monitoring and instant alerts when compliance issues emerge, ensuring you stay ahead of regulations.

Compliance Levels Explained
Level A
Basic Accessibility
The minimum level of conformance, addressing the most critical barriers that prevent access to web content. Essential for all websites.
  • Text alternatives for images
  • Keyboard accessibility
  • Sufficient color contrast
  • Clear page structure
Level AA
Standard Compliance
The recommended level for most organizations and the requirement under ADA. Addresses major accessibility barriers.
  • Enhanced contrast ratios
  • Resizable text up to 200%
  • Multiple navigation methods
  • Error identification and suggestions
Level AAA
Enhanced Accessibility
The highest level of conformance, ideal for specialized applications or organizations prioritizing accessibility leadership.
  • Sign language interpretation
  • Extended audio descriptions
  • Highest contrast requirements
  • Reading level considerations

Color Contrast & Visual Analysis
Comprehensive Color Validation
Color contrast issues are among the most common accessibility violations. WCAG requires minimum contrast ratios of 4.5:1 for normal text and 3:1 for large text at Level AA. Our platform automatically tests every text element against its background, including overlays, gradients, and semi-transparent layers.
The on-page color picker tool lets designers test color combinations in real-time. Click any element to see its current contrast ratio, then adjust colors until they meet requirements. The tool suggests the closest compliant alternatives, preserving your design aesthetic while ensuring readability for users with low vision or color blindness.
Dynamic content poses unique challenges—hover states, active buttons, and JavaScript-rendered elements. WCAG Scanner captures these interactions, testing contrast across all possible states to ensure consistent accessibility throughout the user experience.
Content & Media Validation
Alt Text Verification
Every image must have appropriate alternative text for screen reader users. Our AI-powered system doesn't just check if alt text exists—it evaluates whether the description is meaningful, contextual, and sufficient for understanding. Flags generic phrases like "image" or "photo" that provide no value.
Video Captions & Transcripts
Multimedia content requires synchronized captions for deaf and hard-of-hearing users, plus full transcripts for users who cannot access audio or video at all. We scan video players to verify caption availability, accuracy, and proper synchronization with audio content.
Icon Classification
Icons serve different purposes—some convey meaning and require labels, while others are purely decorative. Our system distinguishes between functional and decorative icons, ensuring meaningful icons have appropriate accessible names while decorative ones are properly hidden from assistive technologies.
Navigation & Interaction Testing
Keyboard Navigation Flow
Many users navigate websites exclusively with keyboards due to motor disabilities or assistive technology use. Our platform maps your entire keyboard navigation flow, identifying unreachable elements, illogical tab orders, and missing focus indicators that make navigation confusing or impossible.
Focus Order Verification
The order in which elements receive keyboard focus must follow a logical, predictable pattern—typically left-to-right, top-to-bottom. We analyze your tab sequence and flag any jumps or unexpected focus shifts that would disorient keyboard users, providing specific recommendations for correcting tab index values.
Tab Trap Detection
Tab traps occur when keyboard focus becomes stuck within a component like a modal or dropdown, with no way to escape back to the main page. These create critical barriers for keyboard users. Our testing identifies all tab traps and provides escape mechanism implementations.
Touch Target Sizing
Mobile and touchscreen users require adequately sized interactive elements—WCAG 2.2 specifies minimum 24×24 CSS pixel targets. We measure every button, link, and control, flagging undersized targets and recommending spacing adjustments to prevent mis-taps and user frustration.
Form Accessibility Excellence
Complete Form Validation
Forms are critical conversion points, yet they're often the most inaccessible parts of websites. Every form field requires a properly associated label that screen readers can announce. Our system verifies programmatic label associations, checking for explicit elements, ARIA labels, and placeholder text misuse.
Error messages must be accessible, clear, and actionable. We test whether errors are programmatically associated with their fields, announced to screen readers, and provide specific guidance for correction rather than vague warnings. Color alone cannot indicate errors—we verify that text indicators or icons accompany any color-based signaling.
Field Validation Testing
Input validation helps all users, but accessibility requires that validation be communicated effectively. We test whether required fields are properly marked, whether format requirements are clearly explained before submission, and whether real-time validation provides helpful feedback without being disruptive to screen reader users.
Complex forms with conditional fields, multi-step wizards, or dynamic content require special attention. Our testing ensures that changes triggered by user input are announced appropriately and that all form states remain accessible throughout the interaction flow.

Global Standards Compliance
Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)
U.S. federal law prohibiting discrimination against individuals with disabilities. Courts have consistently ruled that websites are "places of public accommodation" covered by ADA Title III. Our platform ensures compliance with ADA requirements based on WCAG 2.1 Level AA, the widely accepted standard.
Section 508
Federal regulation requiring U.S. government agencies and contractors to make their electronic and information technology accessible. Updated in 2017 to align with WCAG 2.0 Level AA. Essential for any organization doing business with federal entities.
European Accessibility Act (EAA)
EU directive requiring private sector products and services, including websites and mobile applications, to be accessible. Enforcement begins June 2025. Covers e-commerce, banking, transportation, and other consumer-facing industries operating in European markets.
EN 301 549
European technical standard for ICT accessibility, harmonized with WCAG 2.1. Required for public procurement in EU member states. Our testing verifies compliance with all applicable requirements, helping you meet European market access obligations.

Frequently Asked Questions
Is the free scan really free?
Yes, absolutely. You can scan any website up to 10 pages completely free with no credit card required. You'll receive a full accessibility report showing all detected issues with detailed remediation guidance. This gives you a clear picture of your current compliance status before deciding on a paid plan.
How accurate is automated accessibility testing?
Automated testing reliably catches 70-80% of WCAG violations, including all issues that can be detected programmatically—missing alt text, insufficient color contrast, keyboard accessibility problems, and more. For complete assurance, we recommend combining automated scans with our expert manual audit services, which identify issues requiring human judgment.
What happens if my site goes out of compliance after I fix everything?
That's exactly why we built continuous monitoring. Your site changes constantly—new content, design updates, third-party integrations. Our platform automatically rescans your site on your chosen schedule (daily, weekly, or monthly) and immediately alerts you to any new violations, so you can fix them before they become legal problems.
Do you support non-English websites?
Yes. WCAG Scanner tests websites in any language. Accessibility requirements are largely language-independent—color contrast, keyboard navigation, alt text presence, and structural elements all function the same way regardless of content language. Our reports can be generated in multiple languages for international teams.
Can I use this for client websites if I'm an agency?
Absolutely. Our agency plans include white-label reporting, bulk scanning capabilities, and client management features. Scan multiple client sites from a single dashboard, generate branded reports, and offer accessibility monitoring as a value-added service. Many agencies use WCAG Scanner as a core part of their service offerings.
What level of WCAG compliance do I need?
Most organizations should target WCAG 2.1 Level AA, which is the standard referenced in ADA cases and federal regulations. This provides strong accessibility for users with disabilities while remaining achievable for most websites. Level AAA is optional and recommended only for specialized applications. We'll help you determine the right compliance level for your specific situation.
